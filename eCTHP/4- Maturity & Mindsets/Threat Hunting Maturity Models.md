# Threat Hunting Maturity Models

Threat hunting maturity is a way to measure how consistently and effectively an organization can hunt, learn, and improve.

## Typical measurement factors

- amount and quality of collected data
- frequency and consistency of data collection
- methods used to analyze data
- ability to apply automation in analysis

## Hunting Maturity Model

Many teams use a Hunting Maturity Model with levels from 0 to 4.

Lower maturity environments often:

- depend on ad-hoc hunting
- have uneven data quality
- rely on individual expertise

Higher maturity environments more often:

- run repeatable hunts
- measure outcomes
- convert hunt results into detection and response improvements
