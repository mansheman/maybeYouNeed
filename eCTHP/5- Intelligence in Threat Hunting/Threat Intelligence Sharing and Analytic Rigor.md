# Threat Intelligence Sharing and Analytic Rigor

## Threat intelligence sources and sharing

Main source types:

- OSINT
- commercial feeds
- closed or restricted sources
- ISACs and sector communities

The strongest intelligence usually comes from correlating across multiple sources instead of trusting a single feed.

## STIX, TAXII, and TLP

- STIX = what is shared
- TAXII = how it is shared
- TLP = who it is shared with

These standards make intelligence more structured, automatable, and safer to distribute.

## Structured analytical techniques and cognitive biases

Useful SATs:

- Analysis of Competing Hypotheses
- Key Assumptions Check
- Red Team Analysis
- Timeline analysis
- Link analysis

Biases to watch:

- confirmation bias
- anchoring bias
- mirror imaging
- availability bias
- groupthink
