# Incident Prioritization and Management
[[Tags/SOC]] [[Tags/HTB]] [[Tags/IncidentHandling]]

Effective response requires prioritization and clear leadership.

### Prioritization
Different incidents have different impacts. High severity incidents require immediate attention and resources. Even low-rated suspicious events may require initial investigation to confirm if they are incidents.

### Leadership: The Incident Manager
- Often assigned to a SOC manager, CISO/CIO, or trusted third-party vendor.
- Must have the mandate to direct business units and require timely employee action.
- Single point of communication for tracking investigation activities and completion status.

### Key Resources
- **[NIST Computer Security Incident Handling Guide (SP 800-61r3)](https://nvlpubs.nist.gov/nistpubs/SpecialPublications/NIST.SP.800-61r3.pdf)**: Practical guidelines for effective and efficient response.
